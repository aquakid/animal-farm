Short Summary

Animal Farm is a story about a group of farm animals who overthrow their human owner in the hope of creating an equal and fair society. However, things quickly spiral out of control as the pigs, who are in charge, become just as oppressive as the humans they replaced. Itâ€™s a tale about power, corruption, and how idealism can go wrong.

Main Characters

  • Napoleon: A pig who becomes the dictator of Animal Farm. He starts out promising equality but becomes power-hungry and tyrannical.
  • Snowball: Another pig who initially helps lead the rebellion but is chased away by Napoleon. He represents the more idealistic side of leadership.
  • Boxer: A loyal and hardworking horse who believes that hard work will solve everything. He trusts the pigs blindly.
  • Squealer: A pig who manipulates the other animals with clever words and propaganda, making them believe whatever Napoleon wants them to.
  • Old Major: The old pig whose ideas about rebellion inspire the animals to rise up, but he dies before the revolution happens.

Key Themes

The main themes in Animal Farm are power, corruption, and betrayal. What starts out as a hopeful revolution for equality quickly turns into a dictatorship where the pigs exploit the other animals. The book explores how those in power can manipulate others and how ideals are often twisted by those who seek control.
